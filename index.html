What's in it

Timing tower
Composite-timing leaderboard. Lap-down detection that works mid-track. Tower data column swaps between gap, interval, manufacturer, gain/loss, last lap, last pit, speed, best.
Starting grid
Two-column grid with custom car numbers, helmets, qualifying times. Falls back to driver list when there's no qual.
Stage & race results
Auto-pops stage results at the stage boundary. Race results overlay with full stage + finish + bonus points breakdown the moment the checkered drops.
Pit report
Latches when cars exit pit lane. Stall time, gain/loss in positions, tire stop detection (2T/4T heuristic), tow filtering, speeding-penalty handling. Only shows under caution.
Driver info & stats
Focused-driver lower third. Season stats drawer (starts, wins, top-5, avg finish, points, incidents) sourced from your league standings.
Replay & camera control
Transport controls (slow-mo 1/2 → 1/16, frame step, scrub, rewind, jump-to-live). One-click camera focus on any driver. Saved camera-group profiles per track.
Event viewer
Running log of overtakes, off-tracks, crashes, flag changes. Click a row to jump iRacing's replay to that moment with the camera on the involved car.
OBS refresh
One button refreshes every browser source in every scene via OBS WebSocket. No more digging in Tools menu during a stop set.
Race history
Auto-saves each broadcast's results, stage points, laps led, fastest lap. Browse, tag, and delete past races without leaving the app.

How it fits together

The launcher boots a bundled Python server that reads iRacing's shared memory at 20Hz and broadcasts the live state over WebSocket. Each overlay is a regular browser source pointed at http://localhost:9212. You add the sources to your OBS scene once and leave them there — the launcher controls visibility via config writes. There's no SDK profile to maintain, no OBS Interact mode required during the broadcast.

Things people ask before they buy

Does it work with my existing OBS scenes?

Yes. You add each overlay as a regular browser source pointed at the launcher. You can put them in any scene collection, scene, or group. The launcher toggles visibility from the app side, so you're not constantly switching scenes.

What happens if my internet drops mid-race?

Almost nothing. The launcher talks to iRacing on your PC and serves overlays to OBS on your PC. Internet is only needed for the optional standings scraper and the periodic license re-check (which has a 7-day offline grace period — you will not get locked out during a broadcast).

Can I use my own league branding?

Yes. Logos, custom car number images, helmet liveries, and per-track camera presets all live in folders you control. Drop in your files; the overlays pick them up next session.

How do updates work?

The launcher checks for updates in the background and shows a banner with release notes when a new version is ready. Click "Restart now" or just relaunch later — it installs in place.

Can I move my license to another PC?

Yes. In the app, click "Release this machine," then activate on the new PC with the same key.

Does it work with other sims (rFactor 2, ACC, AMS2)?

No. iRacing only. The timing engine is built against iRacing's shared memory format.
