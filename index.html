What's in it

Timing tower
Live leaderboard. The right column swaps to whatever you want shown: gap, interval, manufacturer logo, gain/loss, last lap, last pit, current speed, best lap. Lap-down detection actually works when cars are mid-track, not just at start/finish.
Starting grid
Two columns of car numbers, helmets, qual times. Slides up from the bottom on the parade lap. Falls back to the driver list if there's no qualifying session.
Stage and race results
Stage results pop up automatically at the end of each stage. Final race results show up the moment the checkered drops, with stage points, finish points, and bonus points already added up.
Pit report
When a car exits pit lane it locks in: stall time, positions gained or lost, 2 tire vs 4 tire stop. Tow-ins and speeding penalties get handled correctly. Only shows under caution, because nobody wants this graphic up under green.
Driver info and stats
Lower third for whichever driver the camera is on. Slide-in stats drawer with their season starts, wins, top-5s, average finish, points, and incidents. Pulled from your league standings.
Replay and camera control
Slow-mo from 1/2 down to 1/16. Frame-by-frame stepping, scrub, rewind, jump back to live. Click any driver in the launcher to switch the iRacing camera to them. Save your camera setups per track so you don't redo them every week.
Event viewer
Live log of every overtake, off-track, crash, and flag change. Click a row and iRacing's replay jumps to that moment with the camera already on the car that caused it.
OBS refresh
One button refreshes every browser source in every scene. No more digging through OBS menus when an overlay glitches mid-broadcast.
Race history
Every race you broadcast gets saved automatically. Results, stage points, laps led, fastest lap. Browse them later, tag them, delete the ones you don't want.

How it actually works

The launcher runs a small Python server that reads iRacing's shared memory 20 times a second. Overlays are just browser sources pointed at http://localhost:9212. You set them up in OBS once, leave them there. The app handles when each overlay shows and when it hides. No SDK profile to maintain. No OBS Interact mode needed during the race.

Things people ask

Does it work with my existing OBS scenes?

Yeah. Each overlay is just a browser source. Put them in whatever scenes you want, in any collection. The app controls when they show, so you're not switching scenes constantly.

What if my internet drops during a race?

Basically nothing happens. The whole thing runs on your PC, so iRacing and OBS keep talking to each other fine. The only stuff that needs internet is the standings scraper (which is optional anyway) and the periodic license check. The license has a 7-day offline buffer so you won't get locked out mid-broadcast.

Can I use my own branding?

Yeah. Drop your league logo, custom car numbers, helmets, and camera presets into the folders the app shows you. Next session, they show up.

How do updates work?

The app checks for new versions in the background. When there's one, you get a banner with what changed. Hit "Restart now" or just quit and relaunch later. Either way, it installs itself.

Can I move my license to another PC?

Yeah. Hit "Release this machine" in the app, then activate on the new PC with the same key. One PC at a time.

Does it work with other sims?

No, iRacing only. The timing engine reads iRacing's shared memory directly. Other sims would need a full rewrite of that piece.
