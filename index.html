What's in it

  1. Timing tower Live leaderboard. The data column swaps between gap, interval, manufacturer, gain/loss, last lap, last pit, speed, best. Lap-down detection that works mid-track, not just at the line.
  2. Starting grid Two columns of car numbers, helmets, qual times. Slides up on the parade lap. Falls back to the driver list if there's no qualifying session.
  3. Stage and race results Stage results pop automatically at each stage boundary. Final results show the moment the checkered drops, with stage, finish, and bonus points already added up.
  4. Pit report Locks in as cars exit pit lane: stall time, positions gained or lost, 2 tire vs 4 tire. Tow-ins and speeding penalties handled. Only shows under caution.
  5. Driver info and stats Lower third for whoever the camera is on, plus a season stats drawer: starts, wins, top 5s, average finish, points, incidents.
  6. Replay and camera control Slow-mo from 1/2 down to 1/16, frame stepping, jump to live. Click any driver to put the iRacing camera on them. Camera setups saved per track.
  7. Event viewer Running log of overtakes, off-tracks, crashes, flags. Click a row and the replay jumps to that moment with the camera already on the right car.
  8. OBS refresh One button refreshes every browser source in every scene. No digging through OBS menus mid-broadcast.
  9. Race history Every race saved automatically: results, stage points, laps led, fastest lap. Browse, tag, delete.

How it actually works

iRacing shared memory ──> bundled server, 20Hz ──> OBS browser sources
                                   |
                              the launcher

The launcher runs a small server that reads iRacing's shared memory 20 times a second. Overlays are just browser sources pointed at localhost. You add them to your scenes once and leave them. The app decides when each overlay shows. No SDK profile to maintain, no OBS Interact mode during the race, and nothing depends on your internet connection.

Things people ask

Does it work with my existing OBS scenes?

Yeah. Each overlay is just a browser source. Put them in whatever scenes you want, in any collection. The app controls when they show, so you're not switching scenes constantly.

What if my internet drops during a race?

Basically nothing happens. The whole thing runs on your PC, so iRacing and OBS keep talking to each other fine. The only stuff that needs internet is the standings scraper (optional anyway) and the periodic license check, which has a 7-day offline buffer so you won't get locked out mid-broadcast.

Can I use my own branding?

Yeah. Drop your league logo, custom car numbers, helmets, and camera presets into the folders the app shows you. Next session, they show up.

How do updates work?

The app checks for new versions in the background. When there's one, you get a banner with what changed. Hit "Restart now" or just quit and relaunch later. Either way, it installs itself.

Can I move my license to another PC?

Yeah. Hit "Release this machine" in the app, then activate on the new PC with the same key. One PC at a time.

Does it work with other sims?

No, iRacing only. The timing engine reads iRacing's shared memory directly. Other sims would need a full rewrite of that piece.
